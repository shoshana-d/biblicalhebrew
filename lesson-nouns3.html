Section heading

As we have already learned, in the Tanakh, the possessive pronouns my, your, his, her, our, their are never separate words, they are always suffixes attached to the end of a noun, for example ben, bno.

Remember, Plural nouns ending in im, im removed before adding suffix, ie suffix added to singular. Plural nouns ending in ot suffix is added to plural, repeat example from Nouns 1

Examples are those which occur in the Tanakh +infrequent occur only once or twice

Next most frequently occurring is "your". Four different sets of suffixes, because Tanakh distinguishes between whether the speaker is talking to one male person , several male persons , one female person or several female persons . Your is the most frequent, followed by your . Your occurs quite infrequently, and your hardly ever.
