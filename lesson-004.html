Order of words in sentences is often verb subject

  • In English the word order is usually subject-verb-object, so that the verb comes after the subject:
    The girl (subject) saw (verb) the cat (object)
  • In Biblical Hebrew the word order is often verb-subject-object, that is, the verb often comes before the subject
    Saw (verb) the girl (subject) the cat (object)
    (although this sentence doesn't occur in the Tanakh because sadly there is no mention of cats in the Tanakh)

An example which does occur many times in the Torah:

Exodus 4:4 And God said to Moses וַיֹּ֤אמֶר יְהוָה֙ אֶל־מֹשֶׁ֔ה tanakh/ex-04-04aa

וַיֹּאמֶרwords/w011_vayomer and וַתֹּאמֶרwords/w011_vatomer both mean "spoke". וַיֹּאמֶרwords/w011_vayomer is used when a male person spoke, וַתֹּאמֶרwords/w011_vatomer is used when a female person spoke.

יְהוָהwords/w062_adonai and אֱלֹהִיםwords/w018_elohim are the most frequently occurring words for God in the Tanakh. See the section on the Tetragrammaton on the Alefbet page for more detail.

אֵל words/w008_el is a preposition meaning "to". It is often joined to the next word with a hyphen.
