If you've decided to learn (or relearn!) Biblical Hebrew, this website is for you.

These pages cover only pronunciation, that is, sounding out words without necessarily understanding their meanings. Pronunciation is really important, pbh in the Tanakh intended to be spoken (actually, sung/chanted) Most of the time I have not provided translations of words used as examples; these pages are not intended to teach vocabulary. And trying to learn vocabulary out of context is unproductive. List of words with translations in alefbet+

Alefbet

The alefbet (consonants) and vowels; how to pronounce words by breaking them into syllables, and which syllable is stressed (emphasised).

Also information about cantillation marks. Worthwhile making the extra effort to learn about these, as they are invaluable in assisting with pronunciation but also later on in understanding punctuation to help decipher the Hebrew text.

challenging + hints

If you are just beginning your Biblical Hebrew journey.. Learning Hebrew, especially if you're new to the Hebrew letters and vowels, can be challenging. Remember, consistency is key. It's better to practice a little each day, even just 10 minutes, rather than infrequent, long bursts. If you're a complete beginner, you might start by selecting one or two letters that interest you and learn them first. Keep at it, do a little each day, frequent small amoutns of practise is better than long bursts every now and then. Set aside some time each day - as little as 10 minutes.

If you are a complete beginner you may like to start by choosing one or two letters which appeal to you and learning those first. My favourites are lamech and shin - for a long time these were the only 2 letters I could recognize in the parayerbook. In alefbet exercises there is an exercise where you can choose one or more consonants and vowels and practise the sounds

If you have never learnt the hebrew letters and vowels be aware that it is challenging.

why this website?

When as an adult I began to learn Biblical Hebrew, [recently]I couldn't find any materials which were exactly what I needed.

wanted to be able to read and understand texts in the Tanakh but happy to use a translation to help with this.

so started creating a website which has what I woudl have liked - learning to recognize the 100 or so most frequently occurring roots (cos these make up 70%?? of the text, and just enough grammar to be able to decipher the text after having read a translation

initially was going to assume that users already knew the alefbet, but remembered how long it took me to learn - also for this couldn't find resources with audio feedbakc and lots of exercises - had several attempts before finally learning in a Yiddish class - thank you Esther Singer!

So thought I would just have a small page, then grew, needed some exercises, added stuff about pronunciation that's often rather buried in the middle of grammar books and it just grew...

This website is my project to create the resource that I would have liked when starting on my Biblical Hebrew journey. [hebrew lashon kodesh important for deepening relationship with judaism, living a jewish life, connecting with the incredibly rich jewish heritage) wanted to provide a resource for people like me who are coming to Biblical Hebrew as adults, perhaps having missed out on a jewish education as chiodren

These current pages are the first part of the project and cover only pronunciation, Audio is a feature of this site [because this is what i needed most and couldn't find].

Have included the things which I found puzzling as a learner.

Second part of the project, learning to read with understanding, will follow (eventually!)

As a person who missed out on a Jewish education as a child, I found learning the hebrew alefbet challenging.
