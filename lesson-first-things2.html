Prefixes (added at the beginning of Hebrew words): וּמֹשֶׁהwords/_xx "and Moses"

Some English words are not separate words in Tanakh Hebrew. In Tanakh Hebrew, these "words" are added as prefixes (at the beginning of a Hebrew word) or suffixes (at the end of a word). For example:

עִיר

city, town

הָעִיר

the city

וְהָעִיר

and the city

מֵהָעִיר

from the city

לָעִיר

to the city

כְּעִיר

like a city

בָּעִיר

in the city

עִירוֹ

his city

בְעִירוֹ

in his city

עִירָם

their city

So a single Hebrew word often corresponds to two or more English words.

Prefixes with -כָּלwords/w010_kol

The word -כָּלwords/w010_kol occurs very frequently in the Tanakh. It is translated as "all" or "every" depending on the following word. It is always attached to the following word with a hyphen.

הָאָרֶץwords/w021_haaretz the land   כָּל־הָאָרֶץ words/w010_kol_haaretz all the land  
הָעָםwords/w027_haam the people   כָּל־הָעָםwords/w027_xxkolhaam all the people  
אִישׁwords/w023_ish (a) man   כָּל־אִישׁ words/w023_xxkolish every man
