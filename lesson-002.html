Prefixes: וּמֹשֶׁהwords/_xx "and Moses"

Some English words are not separate words in Biblical Hebrew. In Biblical Hebrew, these "words" are added as prefixes (at the beginning of a Hebrew word) or suffixes (at the end of a word). For example:

עִיר

city, town

הָעִיר

the city

וְהָעִיר

and the city

מֵהָעִיר

from the city

לָעִיר

to the city

כְּעִיר

like a city

בָּעִיר

in the city

עִירוֹ

his city

בְעִירוֹ

in his city

עִירָם

their city

So a single Hebrew word often corresponds to two or more English words.

Prefixes (at the beginning of a word) כּ בּ ל
