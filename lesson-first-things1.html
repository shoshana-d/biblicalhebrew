Words for God, names of people and places

Names of people and places are called proper nouns. In English, they start with a capital letter (Moses, Egypt) but there are no capital letters in Hebrew. When trying to decipher a verse, the first thing you should look at is whether there are any proper names, and if so, to find then in the Hebrew.

Names of God in the Tanakh

יהוה

In the Tanakh, the word יהוה refers to God. יהוה is the most frequently occurring word on the Tanakh (as you would expect). יהוה is called the Tetragrammaton (which is Greek for "four letters"). Jewish practice is to pronounce this word as אֲדוֹנָיwords/w062_adonai (Adonai, my Lord). יהוה is usually pointed יְהוָה so the vowels are similar to אֲדוֹנָיwords/w062_adonai See How to pronounce יְהוָה the Tetragrammaton in alefbet for more information on the pronunciation of this word.

translations of יהוה God, GOD, the LORD, L-rd, Lord, Adonoy, untranslated, Yhwh

pronouns relating to God (His, Him) also start with a capital letter in most translations
אֱלֹהִיםwords/w018_elohim

אֱלֹהִיםwords/w018_elohim is the next most frequently occurring word for God. More about this word in Lesson 5. When you see the one of the words for God in the translation, you should first check to sse if יהוה is in the Hebrew, and if not, look for אֱלֹהִים

אֲדֹנָיwords/w062_adonai אָדוֺןwords/w062_adon means "lord" and is used to refer to humans as well as to God. When not referring to God usually pointed differently (different vowels) אֲדֹנִיwords/w062_adoni "my lord" My Lord
אֲדֹנָי יֱהוִהalefbet/adonaielohim

Sometimes יהוה and אֲדֹנָיwords/w062_adonai occur together to refer to God. In this case, יהוה is pronounced as though it were written אֱלֹהִיםwords/w018_elohim so the pronunciation is אֲדֹנָי יֱהוִהalefbet/adonaielohim, Adonai Elohim, and יהוה is sometimes pointed with the vowels of Elohim instead אֱלֹהִיםwords/w018_elohim.

Gen 15:2 O Lord GOD (JPS),“O lord יהוה" (JPS 2006), My Lord, YHWH (Fox), O my Sovereign GOD (revised JPS), Lord God
אֵלwords/w188_el El may also refer to other gods, without capital letter
יָהּalefbet/yah Yah הַלְלוּ־יָהּwords/w282_hallelujah halleluyah (praise Yah) most common occurrence?
שַׁדַּיwords/w671_shaddai Almighty, sometimes אֵל שַׁדַּיwords/w671_shaddai El Shaddai, God Almighty

Names of people and places in the Tanakh

Mostly can guess by spelling them out, eg David, Abraham, Sara. Some less obvious ones, Shmuel, Rivkah, maybe Moshe, Mitzraim, hayor
